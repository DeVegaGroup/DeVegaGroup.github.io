# Dr. Jose De Vega: A Journey Through Genomes

[cite_start]My scientific path began in **Salamanca, Spain**, where I earned my PhD in Agronomic Biotechnology, focusing on the molecular interactions of the *Fusarium* wilt in common beans[cite: 1]. [cite_start]This foundational interest in crop resilience has guided my career across Europe, from transcriptomics in Portuguese pines to managing major genome projects for red clover and Miscanthus in the UK[cite: 1].

## Leading the Way
[cite_start]Since 2022, I have served as a **Tenure-Track Group Leader at the Earlham Institute**, where I lead a vibrant team of researchers. My leadership style is built on active mentorship; [cite_start]I have supervised numerous PhD students and PDRAs, many of whom have transitioned into independent research roles or specialized bioinformatics positions at institutions like RBG Kew[cite: 6]. 

## A Global Research Footprint
My work is characterized by high-impact international collaborations. [cite_start]I have led projects funded by **UKRI, GCRF, and Horizon Europe**, totaling millions in research investment[cite: 1]. [cite_start]Whether it's exploring rice landrace diversity in Vietnam or safeguarding banana cultivars in Colombia, my goal is always the same: translating fundamental genomic insights into tangible breeder-ready resources[cite: 3].

## Scholarly Contributions
[cite_start]With a publication record spanning top-tier journals like *Scientific Reports*, *BMC Plant Biology*, and *Annals of Botany*, I have consistently contributed to the advancement of plant genomics[cite: 2]. [cite_start]My recent work includes pioneering the use of machine learning for seed species classification and developing haplotype-resolved assemblies for complex polyploid species like *Urochloa*[cite: 2].
